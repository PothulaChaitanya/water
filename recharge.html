Recharging borewells and wells naturally

Borewell recharging is an environmentally friendly method of rainwater harvesting that uses harvested surface water to replenish groundwater. The process involves passing runoff water through a natural filter made of stones, then through sand, and finally through a fine mesh wrapped around the drilled casing pipe




Here are some steps for recharging a borewell:-

  1. Construct a pond about 20 ft x 15 ft and 8 ft deep near the borewell site to gather runoff water
  2. Dig a pit around the borewell casing that is 6 ft x 4 ft and 8 ft deep
  3. Line the bottom of the pit with filtration material, such as 40 mm stones, 20 mm, and 6 mm in size
  4. Cut slits into the borewell casing using a cutting machine
  1. Wrap the casing with nylon mesh to prevent solids from entering the casing pipe
  2. Channel the water through a narrow pipe to increase the water force
  3. Pass the water through a stone wall for primary filtering
  4. Connect it with drip pipes to ensure the right amount of drip irrigation


Advantages of Recharging borewells and wells naturally:-

  • Water reserve:Borewell recharge creates a water reserve for times of drought
  • Water quality: Borewell recharge improves water quality by storing naturally filtered rainwater in aquifers and water tables.
  • Social impact:Borewell recharge prevents adverse social impacts like displacement of population or loss of agricultural land.
  • Water loss: Borewell recharge minimizes the chances of water loss due to evaporation.
