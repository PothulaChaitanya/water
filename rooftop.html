Rooftop rainwater harvesting

Rooftop rainwater harvesting (RTRWH) is a technique for collecting and storing rainwater that falls on a building's roof. The collected water can be used for watering plants




Steps For Rooftop rainwater harvesting:-

  1. Collect rainwater from rooftops using PVC pipes.
  2. Filter the collected water with sand and bricks.
  3. Transport the water through underground pipes to a sump for immediate use.
  4. Move excess water from the sump to a well.
  5. Use the water in the well to recharge underground water.


Advantages of rooftop rainwater harvesting:-

  • Less cost.
  • Helps in reducing the water bill.
  • Decreases the demand for water.
  • Reduces the need for imported water
  • Promotes both water and energy conservation.
  • Improves the quality and quantity of groundwater.
  • Does not require a filtration system for landscape irrigation.
  • This technology is relatively simple, easy to install and operate.
  • It reduces soil erosion, stormwater runoff, flooding.
